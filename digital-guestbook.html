The problem with paper guestbooks

Paper guestbooks feel romantic — until three months later, when you can't read half the messages, the photos were separate on someone else's phone, and the book is on a shelf gathering dust.

Your guests want to contribute something real. A digital guestbook lets them post a photo of themselves at the table, leave a message that appears on the big screen in real time, and have it all saved — readable, shareable, permanent — the moment the event ends.

The choice isn't paper versus digital. It's whether the memories your guests make actually survive the night.

Questions

Is a digital guestbook better than a paper one?
It depends what you want to keep. Paper is tactile but messages get lost, photos can't be added, and the book sits in a drawer. A digital guestbook keeps everything — photos, messages, audio — in one place you can revisit any time.
Do guests need an account or app?
No. GatherWall guests scan a QR code with any camera app and post instantly — no download, no sign-up.

Everything is saved. You get a download of every photo and message your guests posted.

Absolutely. Many couples do — a paper book for signatures and a GatherWall wall for photos, messages, and the live big-screen moment.
